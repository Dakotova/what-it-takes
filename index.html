Description

By the chairman, CEO, and co-founder of Blackstone, Stephen A. Schwarzman, a long-awaited book that uses surprising events from the life of Schwarzman to teach people how to create, improve and lead thriving organizations. Whether you are a student, an investor, a philanthropist, an executive or just someone who is searching for ways to improve your ability, the same lessons apply.

 

People know who Stephen Schwarzman is — they believe they do at least. He’s the one who took $400,000 and co-founded Blackstone, the $500 billion investment company (as of January 2019). He’s the CEO, whose opinions the heads of state are finding. He’s the billionaire philanthropist who created Schwarzman Scholars in China, the variant of this century’s Rhodes Scholarship. But behind those accomplishments is a man who has spent his life studying and thinking about what it takes to achieve success, make an impact, and live a meaningful life.

 

Folding handkerchiefs in a linen shop for his father, Schwarzman dreamed of a greater life, full of meaning and adventure. His grades and fitness had taken him to Yale. After beginning his finance career with a brief stint at a financial firm called DLJ, Schwarzman began working at Lehman Brothers where he moved up to manage the department of mergers and acquisitions. He eventually joined in founding Blackstone with his mentor and friend Pete Peterson, vowing to build a modern, different kind of financial institution.

 

It has not come easily today to transform Blackstone into the world’s leading financial institution. Schwarzman concentrated intensively on culture, recruiting great talent, and setting up processes that allow the organization to regularly evaluate and assess risk. Schwarzman’s simplistic “do not lose money” slogan has led Blackstone to become the world’s largest private equity and real estate investor, and manager of alternative assets for institutional investors. Both he and the company are known for the rigor of their investment strategy, their creative deal-making approach, the diversification of their business lines, and the belief that they are the best at everything they do.

 

Schwarzman is also an influential philanthropist, giving away more than $1 billion. In philanthropy, as in business, it is drawn to situations where it is possible to apply its capital and energy to push transformative solutions and change paradigms, especially in education. He uses the expertise he has learned in finance over a lifetime to plan, develop and support creative and impactful organizations and initiatives. His gifts ranged from the development of a new College of Computing at the MIT to the study of artificial intelligence, the establishment of a first-class student and performing arts center in Yale, the renovation of the iconic New York Public Library, and the establishing of the Schwarzman Scholars Fellowship Program at the University of Tsinghua in Beijing, China’s largest philanthropic initiative.

 

The tale of Schwarzman is an inspiring, entertaining and insightful guide for everyone who wants greater personal impact. From deal-making to investing, strategy to entrepreneurship, philanthropy to diplomacy, Schwarzman has lessons on how to think about ambition and size, challenges and opportunities, and how to achieve success by seeking excellence without yielding. Not only does Schwarzman give readers a reflective perspective on all his own observations, but he also provides a realistic roadmap for success in doing so.
